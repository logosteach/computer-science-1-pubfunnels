Unit 8 - Algorithms

This unit contains the following lessons:

  • Lesson 1: Introduction to Algorithms and Problem Solving
  • Lesson 2: Measuring Efficiency with Big O Notation
  • Lesson 3: Linear Search
  • Lesson 4: Binary Search
  • Lesson 5: Basic Sorting Algorithms - Bubble Sort and Selection Sort
  • Lesson 6: Faster Sorting - Quick Sort
  • Lesson 7: Jump Search and Algorithm Comparison

Lesson 1 Objectives

By the end of the lesson, students will be able to:

  • explain what an algorithm is and why good algorithms matter in programming.
  • describe the difference between a good solution and an efficient solution.
  • understand that algorithms are step-by-step procedures for solving problems.
  • reflect on how God is a God of order and wisdom, and He calls us to approach problems with thoughtful, orderly methods rather than chaos (1 Corinthians 14:40; Proverbs 3:5-6).

Lesson 2 Objectives

By the end of the lesson, students will be able to:

  • explain what Big O Notation is and why we use it to measure efficiency.
  • identify common time complexities: O(1), O(log n), O(n), O(n log n), and O(n²).
  • understand the difference between best, average, and worst-case scenarios.
  • analyze simple algorithms using Big O notation.
  • recognize that just as we measure efficiency in code, Scripture encourages us to use our time and resources wisely (Ephesians 5:15-16; Psalm 90:12).

Lesson 3 Objectives

By the end of the lesson, students will be able to:

  • implement and explain the Linear Search algorithm.
  • analyze the time complexity of Linear Search (O(n)).
  • understand when Linear Search is appropriate to use.
  • compare the performance of Linear Search on sorted vs unsorted data.
  • reflect on how Linear Search is like carefully examining each option until the right one is found, similar to how we are called to seek truth diligently (Proverbs 2:4-5; Matthew 7:7).

Lesson 4 Objectives

By the end of the lesson, students will be able to:

  • implement and explain the Binary Search algorithm.
  • understand that Binary Search requires sorted data.
  • analyze the time complexity of Binary Search (O(log n)).
  • compare Binary Search with Linear Search in terms of efficiency.
  • recognize that Binary Search illustrates the power of dividing problems in half — a principle that often leads to much faster solutions, just as wisdom helps us divide complex life decisions effectively (Proverbs 16:16).

Lesson 5 Objectives

By the end of the lesson, students will be able to:

  • implement Bubble Sort and Selection Sort.
  • explain how each sorting algorithm works step by step.
  • analyze the time complexity of both algorithms (O(n²)).
  • understand the strengths and weaknesses of these basic sorting methods.
  • reflect on how repeated small improvements (like in Bubble Sort) can eventually lead to order, reminding us of the gradual process of sanctification in the Christian life (Philippians 1:6; 2 Corinthians 3:18).

Lesson 6 Objectives

By the end of the lesson, students will be able to:

  • understand and implement the Quick Sort algorithm (using recursion or built-in understanding).
  • explain why Quick Sort is generally much faster than Bubble or Selection Sort.
  • analyze the average and worst-case time complexity of Quick Sort (O(n log n) average).
  • compare Quick Sort with the slower sorting algorithms.
  • recognize that different problems require different tools — just as God equips each believer with different gifts for different purposes (Romans 12:6; 1 Corinthians 12:4-6).

Lesson 7 Objectives

By the end of the lesson, students will be able to:

  • implement Jump Search and understand when it is useful.
  • compare the efficiency of Linear Search, Binary Search, Jump Search, Bubble Sort, Selection Sort, and Quick Sort.
  • choose the most appropriate algorithm for a given problem and data set.
  • discuss trade-offs between simplicity and performance.
  • reflect on how studying algorithms teaches us wisdom in choosing the best approach — a skill that mirrors the biblical call to walk wisely and make the most of every opportunity (Ephesians 5:15-16; Proverbs 4:7).
