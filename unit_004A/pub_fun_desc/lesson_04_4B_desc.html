Nested IF Blocks

In this lesson, we will explore the concept of nested if statements in programming. Nested if statements allow us to check multiple conditions within each other, enabling more complex decision-making in our code. We will learn how to create and trace nested if statements, understand their usefulness, and avoid making our nested blocks to logically complex.

"The heart of man plans his way, but the Lord establishes his steps." - Proverbs 16:9 (ESV). Knowing the Lord does not remove the complexities of life. This world is still an upward struggle. Knowing the Lord means we have peace with God, therefore we have the ultimate motivation and peace and hope to endure all of life's complexities. (Romans 8:28)

Objectives

By the end of the lesson, learners will be able to:

  • Create and trace nested if statements (if inside another if).

  • Understand when nested conditionals are useful versus when compound conditions are better.

  • Avoid overly complex nesting that makes code hard to read.

  • Debug nested conditional logic.

  • Reflect on how nested decisions remind us that God often works through layers of circumstances while still guiding us with clarity and purpose (Proverbs 16:9; Romans 8:28).
