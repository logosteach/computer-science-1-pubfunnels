The Magnificent Print Function

In this lesson, we will explore the print() function in Python, which is one of the most fundamental and widely used functions in programming. The print() function is pretty basic. It allows us to display output on the screen/display which is essential for communicating with users, debugging our code, and providing us with feedback. You will have a sold understanding of how to use the print() function effectively by the end of this lesson. As we focus on the Lord Jesus together, we will reflect on the importance of clear and intentional communication in our code, but more importantly, in our lives as believers. We are becoming more like Christ by the graces He works into us. He is clear and purposeful in all that He does and is, and He is making us this way more and more by the power of His Spirit working in us.

Objectives

By the end of the lesson, learners will be able to:

  • Explain the purpose and importance of the print() function in Python.
  • Use the print() function to output text, numbers, and combinations of both.
  • Use escape sequences such as \n (newline) and \t (tab) within print() statements.
  • Concatenate strings and numbers using the + operator or commas in print().
  • Create simple, formatted output messages using the print() function.
  • Explain the importance of clear and intentional output in programming and draw a parallel to the biblical command to let our speech and actions be gracious, clear, and purposeful (Colossians 4:6).
