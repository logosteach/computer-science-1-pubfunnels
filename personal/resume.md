**STATEMENT OF FAITH**

I am a Reformed Baptist who holds to the doctrines of grace. I currently serve and worship at Pilgrim Orthodox Presbyterian Church in Bangor, Maine.  

I am called by the Lord Jesus Christ to love His Church—all who are united to Him by faith alone, by grace alone, in Christ alone.  

I affirm as true and unchangeable the following:  

There is one and only one living and true God—eternal, uncreated, and the source of all things. In Him all things have their being and move, and existence proceeds from Him, not the other way around. This is consistent with God’s own declaration of His name and character to Moses at the burning bush (**Exodus 3:14**). God exists eternally as one God in three Persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.  

I believe in the virgin birth of Jesus Christ. He was conceived by the Holy Spirit and born of the Virgin Mary—fully God and fully man.  

God’s plan of salvation has always been His primary plan for mankind. God freely created Adam, who is fully responsible for his sin; God is in no way culpable for Adam’s fall. It was God’s gracious love that initiated the work of redemption, which culminated in the birth, ministry, death, and resurrection of His Son, Jesus Christ.  

**Jesus Christ is the Gospel**—He is the good news. In His incarnation, He was born of a virgin and grew into manhood. He lived in perfect obedience to the Father and willingly submitted to death on the cross, atoning for the sins of all who believe in Him. He rose from the dead, ascended to the right hand of God where He intercedes for His Church, and will return in the flesh to bring resurrection and eternal life to all who believe, and judgment to those who do not.  

At regeneration, the believer receives faith to believe the gospel, a continuing desire to repent of sin, the power to mortify sin, and the certain hope of future glorification. I believe that once God begins His work of salvation in a believer’s heart, He will faithfully complete it. This work depends entirely upon God’s grace; yet believers are not excused from pursuing holiness and righteousness in Christ—a pursuit motivated by love and gratitude rather than mere duty.  

I fully embrace the **Five Solas of the Reformation**:  
- **Sola Scriptura** – Scripture alone is the ultimate authority.  
- **Sola Gratia** – Salvation is by grace alone.  
- **Sola Fide** – Salvation is through faith alone.  
- **Solus Christus** – Salvation is in Christ alone.  
- **Soli Deo Gloria** – All for the glory of God alone.
