Python Lists

In this lesson, we will explore Python lists, which are a fundamental data structure that allows us to store and manipulate collections of items. Lists are versatile and can hold items of different types, making them an essential tool for any Python programmer. We will cover how to create lists, access and modify their elements, and utilize various built-in methods to work with them effectively.

If you have been a member of a local church for long, you will notice that the Lord has a curious collection of peculiar people. Perhaps you and I are the truly peculiar ones. Yet, none-the-less God has gathered us together into a community, united to His Son, for the purpose of rejoicing, glorifying, and simply basking in His love for us. In a similar way, lists in Python allow us to gather together different items into a single collection that we can work with. (1 Corinthians 12:12-27; Ephesians 2:19-22)

Objectives

  • create and work with lists in Python.

  • access, modify, and add elements to a list using indexing and methods like append(), insert(), and remove().

  • use built-in functions such as len(), min(), max(), and sum() with lists.

  • iterate through a list using a for loop.

  • recognize that just as lists allow us to organize and work with collections of data, God gathers His people into communities and families where each person has value and purpose (Romans 12:4-5; Ephesians 4:16).
