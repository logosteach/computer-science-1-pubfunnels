Nested Loops

In this lesson, we will explore the concept of nested loops in Python. Nested loops are loops that exist within another loop. They allow us to perform more complex iterations and can be particularly useful when working with multi-dimensional data structures, such as lists of lists (2D arrays) or when we need to compare elements in a collection against each other.

Joseph's brothers hated him. They were going to kill him but instead they sold him for money to some traders passing through. God had multiple purposes in allowing this to happen to Joseph. From slave to head man in the house of Potipher, to slave again, to head master of the jail and then second to only Pharaoh of Egypt, God's purposes unfolded in Joseph's life to give God all the glory and work His plan of salvation. To bring His people out of slavery and into the land of promise. How important was it that Israel become slaves first before they could be free and possess the land of promise? Why was this the path that God chose for them? The answers to this are found in Christ Jesus and what He has done for us by bringing us back to God from death and sin. Our former slavery is now over and done with. We are truly free, if we are trusting in Christ for the washing away of our sins! (Galatians 5:1; Romans 6:6-7).

Objectives

By the end of the lesson, learners will be able to:

  • Create and understand nested for and while loops.

  • Use nested loops to solve problems such as printing patterns, tables, or grids.

  • Trace the execution of nested loops (outer vs. inner loop).

  • Avoid common pitfalls such as excessive nesting or off-by-one errors.

  • Reflect on how nested loops show that God often works in layers — He has purposes within purposes — and His plans are beautifully intricate and orderly (Jeremiah 29:11; Romans 11:33-34).
