Python Loops - Unit Objectives

Review these learning objectives before beginning each lesson in the unit.

Lesson 001 - Introduction to Loops

  • Explain why repetition is necessary in programming and give real-world examples of repetitive tasks.

  • Describe the two main types of loops in Python: while and for.

  • Understand the basic concept of iteration and how loops execute code repeatedly.

  • Recognize the danger of infinite loops and how to prevent them.

  • Reflect on how loops illustrate the importance of rhythm and order in God’s creation, just as seasons, day and night, and the cycles of life show His faithful and consistent character (Genesis 8:22; Psalm 104:19).

Lesson 002 - The While Loop

  • Write and understand the syntax of a while loop.

  • Use a while loop with a counter or condition that eventually becomes false.

  • Combine while loops with user input to create interactive programs.

  • Identify and avoid infinite loops by ensuring the loop condition eventually changes.

  • Reflect on how the while loop reminds us that persistence in prayer and faithfulness is important, yet we must also know when to stop and trust God’s timing (Luke 18:1-8; Ecclesiastes 3:1).

Lesson 003 - The For Loop and the range() Function

  • Write and understand the syntax of a for loop.

  • Use the range() function with start, stop, and step parameters.

  • Iterate over sequences such as strings and simple lists.

  • Explain when to use a for loop versus a while loop.

  • Recognize that just as the for loop allows us to move through a known sequence step by step, God leads us one step at a time through life, guiding our path with His Word (Psalm 119:105; Proverbs 3:5-6).

Lesson 004 - Loop Control Statements: Break and Continue

  • Use the break statement to exit a loop early.

  • Use the continue statement to skip the rest of the current iteration.

  • Apply break and continue to solve practical programming problems.

  • Understand how these statements affect loop execution.

  • Reflect on how break and continue illustrate the biblical ideas of repentance (stopping wrong behavior) and pressing on toward the goal in Christ (Philippians 3:13-14).

Lesson 005 - The Pass Statement and Loop Else Clause

  • Use the pass statement as a placeholder in loops and conditional blocks.

  • Understand and use the optional else clause with for and while loops.

  • Explain when the else clause executes (when no break occurs).

  • Write cleaner code using these advanced loop features when appropriate.

  • Recognize that just as the else clause runs only when a loop completes normally, God’s promises are “Yes” and “Amen” when we remain faithful and do not quit prematurely (2 Corinthians 1:20).

Lesson 006 - Nested Loops

  • Create and understand nested for and while loops.

  • Use nested loops to solve problems such as printing patterns, tables, or grids.

  • Trace the execution of nested loops (outer vs. inner loop).

  • Avoid common pitfalls such as excessive nesting or off-by-one errors.

  • Reflect on how nested loops show that God often works in layers — He has purposes within purposes — and His plans are beautifully intricate and orderly (Jeremiah 29:11; Romans 11:33-34).

Lesson 007 - Putting It All Together: Loops Best Practices

  • Choose the most appropriate loop (for or while) for different situations.

  • Identify and fix common loop errors, including infinite loops and off-by-one errors.

  • Debug loops using print statements and logical thinking.

  • Write clean, readable, and efficient loop code.

  • Reflect on how mastering loops teaches us the value of diligence, patience, and doing all things “decently and in order,” as the Scriptures encourage (1 Corinthians 14:40; Colossians 3:23).
