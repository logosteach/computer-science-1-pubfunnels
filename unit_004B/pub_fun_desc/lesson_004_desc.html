Loop Control Statements: Break and Continue

In this lesson, we will explore two important loop control statements in Python: break and continue. These statements allow us to alter the flow or our loops. The break statement is used to exit a loop prematurely when a certain condition is met, while the continue statement skips the current iteration and moves on to the next one.

Lost in sin and disobedience, we were trapped in a loop of rebellion against the living and true God. But praise be to God for His great mercy in Christ Jesus. He has broken the cycle of death that was on our lives, and grafted us into the vine that is Christ. Because we are now in Him, we are part of His life. His cycle is an eternal, life giving cycle that will never break. When we falter in this world, we are allowed to continue, and begin again, for all who go to Him for forgiveness will find the wells of mercy deep and unfathomable. (1 John 1:9)

Objectives

By the end of the lesson, learners will be able to:

  • Use the break statement to exit a loop early.

  • Use the continue statement to skip the rest of the current iteration.

  • Apply break and continue to solve practical programming problems.

  • Understand how these statements affect loop execution.

  • Reflect on how break and continue illustrate the biblical ideas of repentance (stopping wrong behavior) and pressing on toward the goal in Christ (Philippians 3:13-14).
